What is finance?

Finance is a set of economic relations that arise in the process of creating, as well as using funds (centralized or decentralized) funds used for the needs of the state and providing conditions for expanding production. The emergence of finance is associated with the presence of such conditions as regular commodity-money exchange and the need for the state to attract additional resources. There is no one correct definition of finance, fixed in regulatory documents, however, there are several definitions of the concept of "finance", united in one: finance is associated with the formation, distribution and use of monetary funds and the purpose of their existence is to perform the functions and tasks of the state. However, you can find out what finance is from such public regulatory sources as: Civil, Labor, Tax, Budget, and Family Codes.

In the Western scientific and educational literature, general definitions of finance are usually not given, finance is interpreted quite widely. It is usually specified which finances are being discussed: public, corporate or personal finance.
