Saving your finances-the basics

What is finance?

Finance is a set of economic relations that arise in the process of creating, as well as using funds (centralized or decentralized) funds used for the needs of the state and providing conditions for expanding production. The emergence of finance is associated with the presence of such conditions as regular commodity-money exchange and the need for the state to attract additional resources. There is no one correct definition of finance, fixed in regulatory documents, however, there are several definitions of the concept of "finance", united in one: finance is associated with the formation, distribution and use of monetary funds and the purpose of their existence is to perform the functions and tasks of the state. However, you can find out what finance is from such public regulatory sources as: Civil, Labor, Tax, Budget, and Family Codes.

In the Western scientific and educational literature, general definitions of finance are usually not given, finance is interpreted quite widely. It is usually specified which finances are being discussed: public, corporate or personal finance.

What is finance from a household point of view

If we consider the term finance from a household point of view, then first of all this concept is called money – both cash and non-cash (money in bank accounts and in the form of securities). The origin of the term "finance" is due to the French language, in which the word "finance" has a literal translation as "money". And although the definitions of finance are mainly formed around money, finance itself can include other assets, including: precious stones, metals, enterprises, real estate, automobile transport.

Considering the term finance from a broad point of view, it is necessary to take into account the following components: state "public" finance and personal finance, the world financial system and international financial relations, the financial market and credit relations, commercial and civil finance, etc.The narrower meaning of the concept moves to the sphere of direct monetary circulation at the stage of formation and application of funds aimed at solving the tasks of state management, mainly budget funds. The financial system also expresses the relationship between individuals and legal entities.

What is financial literacy?

Financial literacy is the ability of a person to manage their income and expenses, make the right decisions on the distribution of funds (live within their means) and competently multiply them. In other words – it is the knowledge that allows you to achieve financial well-being and stay at this level all your life. Financially literate people do not live from paycheck to paycheck, and take out loans only in cases when they are sure that the loan taken will bring profit in the future. They do not give in to panic even in the conditions of an economic crisis, because they have a "reserve fund" in case of various force majeure.

Unfortunately, the population of our country cannot be called financially literate, since most of them grew up in a completely different era and are simply not used to thinking about financial well-being in the future and planning the receipts and expenditures of funds on a long-term basis. The generation of citizens who grew up in the times cannot teach children and teenagers all the nuances of the world of finance, because they themselves know almost nothing about this field of activity. There was no stock market, money could be stored only in one bank, and the only legitimate source of existence was a salary. And this is evidenced by the following facts:

Almost half of the inhabitants of our country keep money in the old-fashioned way at home. We are not used to increasing our capital, and numerous collapses and crises have only strengthened distrust of financial institutions.

More than half do not use all kinds of financial services. And not because there is no such need. It's just that people don't understand how it all works and don't even know about their existence.
Less than half of the population knows about the deposit insurance system.

Most of the population does not understand how the pension system works (and after all, we are offered to invest in it in order to receive a decent pension in the future).
In the modern world, it is simply necessary to learn the correct handling of money. There is such an expression – money loves an account. This means that it is necessary not only to count the salary or calculate the change. This is taking into account all your expenses and planning them, you can even say planning your life, because everything is sold and everything is bought in it. So we gradually came to teaching the basics of financial literacy.

Develop the right financial habits for yourself

For many people, developing just four useful financial habits may be enough for their financial situation to change for the better. Here are these 4 useful habits:

Start keeping records of income and expenses. Plan your expenses for the month ahead.
Avoid debt and loans – live within your means.
Always immediately after receiving income, save and invest at least 10% of the amount received. And from the remaining money, pay for your usual expenses, starting with the most important ones.
Be sure to consult with professionals before investing money in any investment project.

We strongly recommend using this advice to improve financial literacy. It is not as simple as all the previous ones, as it requires more real effort from you and changes in your usual lifestyle. But you will see the effect of it immediately: Your well-being will increase along with the level of your financial literacy.
